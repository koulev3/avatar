# 2026-04-14 Cinders at Low Tide

- Duration: 3 hours
- Focus: A harbor labor dispute becomes a proxy contest over law, legitimacy, and fear.
- Escalation Level: 3

## Strong Start (In Media Res)
Low-tide bells are still ringing when cargo hooks slam onto the same Fire Nation tea barge twice in a row. Dockworkers shout over one another as a local magistrate unrolls a trade decree and a masked teenager paints a tea bowl sigil on the pier in ash-black ink.

A runner spots the PCs and yells that someone has locked children inside the tally house to force the laborers back to work.

## Scene Pressures
1. **Tally House Lock-In**
   - Urgency: smoke from a knocked-over brazier is building inside.
   - About to go wrong: someone blames the Fire Nation crew before facts are known.
   - Pressures:
     - Rescue first vs identify culprit first.
     - Public anger hardens if the PCs act with force.
     - A witness swears the lock was set by an Earth Kingdom foreman.

2. **Magistrate's Decree Reading**
   - Urgency: decree activates at sundown unless formally contested.
   - About to go wrong: merchants hire private enforcers to "stabilize" the docks.
   - Pressures:
     - Legal challenge requires cooperation with corrupt officials.
     - Public confrontation raises PC reputation as agitators.
     - Quiet compromise preserves peace but legitimizes coercion.

3. **Ash Sigil Rumor Spiral**
   - Urgency: rumors spread that the sigil marks collaborators for retribution.
   - About to go wrong: retaliation list appears before midnight.
   - Pressures:
     - Unmasking the painter may trigger mob justice.
     - Ignoring the sigil invites panic and false accusations.
     - A rebel cell claims the mark to recruit from fear.

## Important NPCs
- `Magistrate Huan` (Earth Kingdom official)
  - Public role: harbor magistrate and decree custodian.
  - Private motive: avoid direct blame from both nations.
  - Wants now: visible PC cooperation to legitimize proceedings.
  - If ignored: authorizes private enforcers to restore order.

- `Officer Natsuru` (Fire Nation logistics officer)
  - Public role: trade security liaison.
  - Private motive: normalize Fire Nation legal framing in local courts.
  - Wants now: PCs to denounce "unlawful interference" on record.
  - If ignored: escalates to embargo threats and selective inspections.

- `Old Fen` (dock union elder)
  - Public role: labor speaker and tea-house mediator.
  - Private motive: keep children safe, even if it means surrendering leverage.
  - Wants now: PCs to force a short truce.
  - If ignored: calls an unsanctioned strike that fractures local support.

## Consequences Tracker
- Immediate consequence: whichever side controls the rescue narrative controls tonight's crowd.
- Delayed consequence: next session opens with either a legal hearing or a crackdown checkpoint.
- Hidden consequence: an outside broker is paying both agitators and peacekeepers to prolong instability.

## PC Moral Pressure
- Justice vs Mercy: expose decree abuse now, or preserve fragile calm.
- Action vs Restraint: intervene physically in crowd control, or risk escalation through delay.
- Loyalty vs Truth: protect a known ally tied to the ash sigil, or reveal their involvement.

## Tea Incident Echo
A tea service set is publicly smashed during negotiations as a statement that "respect has no market value." The act is copied by teenagers across three nearby villages before dawn.

## GM Move Options
- Show the cost of delay: conditions worsen one step whenever the table hesitates.
- Separate them: split urgent rescue and political confrontation.
- Put someone in a spot: ask who takes public responsibility for a no-win call.
